Tanmay Ghai

I am a software engineer and tech lead at X (formerly known as Twitter) working on a variety of things ranging from core services, security infrastructure & product security. In early 2023, I was part of the core project team that brought v1 of Encrypted DM's on the platform to life.

I completed my master's from the Viterbi school of engineering at the University of Southern California in December 2021 and my bachelor's from the University of California, Berkeley in May 2019.

I received the 2022 CSCI Best Research Award for my work in the area of privacy-preserving entity resolution with fully homomorphic encryption. An article profiling the my research and the award can be found here.

Research

My (broad) research interests are in privacy-preserving technologies and secure systems, particularly at the intersection of scalable distributed computing, data privacy, and machine learning. My DBLP entry (CS bibliography) can be found here.

While I was a master's student, I was a Graduate Research Assistant at the Information Sciences Institute, advised by Prof. Srivatsan Ravi. After graduating, I continued on as a visiting researcher at ISI until August 2023.
